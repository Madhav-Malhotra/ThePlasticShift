Where to learn more about Plastic Pollution

"Plastic Pollution" (Our World in Data, 2018)

This is one of the best overviews of the entire plastic pollution issue, with details on everything from microplastics' effects on health to the geographic distribution of plastic waste.
"Plastics and Sustainability" (American Chemistry Council, 2016)

This report analyses the environmental impacts of plastic production (categorised by different applications of plastic) and analyses potential strategies to prevent plastic usage.
"FAQs on Plastics" (Our World in Data, 2018)

The team at Our World in Data created an article answering frequently asked questions on plastic pollution, including questions like "Are plastic straws a big deal?"
"El Dorado of Chemical Recycling" (Zero Waste Europe, 2019)

This report analyses various technologies being developed for chemical recyling (an alternative to conventional mechanical recycling), as well as their use case and environmental impacts.
"Stemming the Tide" (Ocean Conservancy, 2015)

Ocean Conservancy's report explores challenges with informal waste management in developing countries (like with open dumps and waste collectors) and recommends potential solutions.
"Throwing Away the Future" (Greenpeace, 2019)

This report evaluates typical 'solutions' to plastic pollution (like alternative packaging materials and new recycling technology) and why these won't address the issue in the long term.
"Incineration Versus Recycling" (Nate Seltenrich, 2013)

This article examines the debate about which waste management technology should be adopted in Europe, highlighting their benefits and drawbacks.
"The Informal Waste Sector" (Siddharth Hande, 2019)

This paper discusses the lifestyle of informal waste collectors, the role they play in the waste management sector in developing countries, and the challenges they face.
"The Informal Recycling Sector in Developing Countries" (Martin Medina, 2008)

This report discusses the development of solutions for informal waste management (like organised microenterprises) over time in different world regions.
"What a Waste 2.0" (Kaza et al., 2018)

This World Bank report explores insights in solid waste management as a whole, predicting trends in the collection and generation of subcategories (like plastic waste).
"Making Building Materials from Waste" (Sourav Dey, 2018)

This thesis explores the potential of developing construction materials from waste materials like plastics, especially for use in developing countries.
"Life Cyle Assesment of Grocery Carrier Bags" (Bisinella et al., 2018)

This report from the Danish EPA compares the benefits and drawbacks of alternative packaging materials to replace plastic grocery bags.
"Perceptions and Realities of Recycling" (Drew DeSilver, 2016)

This article analyses findings (like trends in access to recycling collection by geography) from a Pew Research Center survey on consumer perceptions of recycling.
"Rethinking the Future of Plastics" (The Ellen MacArthur Foundation, 2016)

This report examines issues that come from plastics usage (especially for packaging) and explores potential solutions like reuse and compostable packaging.
"The Plastics Pipeline" (Beth Gardiner, 2019)

This article discusses the connection between the petrochemical industry and recycling industry, predicting increased plastic production in the future.
"2018 Annual Report" (Recycle BC, 2018)

This report highlights improvements in the performance of businesses in British Columbia when it comes to extended producer responsibility legislation.

We hope these resources prove useful to other problem solvers working to make an impact on plastic pollution!
